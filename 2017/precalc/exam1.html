Equation forms:
  1. slope-intercept: \(y=mx+b\)
  2. point-slope: \(y - y_1 = m(x-x_1)\)
  3. standard: \(y = ax + by + c\)
The \(\measuredangle\) of Inclination of a line is the \(\measuredangle\) of the line and the x-axis. It is measured counterclockwise from the x-axis to the line. \((0 \leq \theta < 180)\) The formula is
$$ m = tan( \theta) $$ We need to brush up on our trigonometry. The tan addition formula is
$$ tan(A+B) = {tan(A) + tan(B) \over 1 - tan(A)*tan(B)} $$ The tan subtraction formula is $$ tan(A-B) = {(tan(A) - tan(B) \over 1 + tan(A)tan(B)} $$ Using the tan formulas we can delve into a proof with geometry that allows us to find the angle between two lines faster. The formula derived in the case that 2 non-perpendicular lines have slopes $m_1$ and $m_2$ the acute $\measuredangle$ between the 2 lines is given by $$ tan(\theta) = \mid{(m_2-m_1)\over(1+m_1*m_2)}\mid $$ Using the distance formula and tangent rules, we can again derive another formula. The distance d from the point $x_0,y_0$ to the line $y=mx+b$ is $$ d = {\mid mx_0+b-y_0\mid\over\sqrt{1+m^2}} $$ We can apply this same formula to the standard form of an equation, and get the formula in the form of $$ d = {\mid Ax_0+By_0+C\mid\over \sqrt{(A^2+B^2)}} $$

Conic Sections

Conic Sections- A conic is the curve formed by the intersection of a plane and a double-mapped cone

Circle- The set of all points in the plane that are a given distance (radius) from a given point (center)
Equation of a Circle: $$ (x-h)^2+(y-k)^2 = r^2 $$ where $(h,k)$ is the center. The circumcenter is the point of concurrency for the circle (i.e. where the $\perp$ bisectors intersect)
